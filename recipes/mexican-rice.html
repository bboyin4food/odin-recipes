Mexican Rice

Description

No Mexican dish is complete without a well seasoned side of fluffy red rice. This is a recipe I have been working on for some time, and have worked it down to a science at this point. I have always struggled to get the rice to the pefect consistency and have found the trick is in having the correct amount of liquid and the right amount of heat. Aside from that, feel free to experiement with ingredients as you see fit for flavor and spice"

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbls Butter
  • 1 Small onion of your choice
  • 1 Tbls Knorr chicken bullion powder or similar
  • 1 1/3 water
  • 1 Cup long grain rice
  • 2/3 Cup Tomato sauce
  • 1-2 cloves of arlic
  • Ground pepper to taste

Optional:

  • Lime
  • Cilantro
  • Jalapeno
  • Celery
  • Peas
  • Carrots

Directions:

  1. Rinse rice until clean and then strain and dry well
  2. In a bowl or large measuring cup, mix together water, chicken bullion and tomato sauce and set aside. The total amount of liquid should be just about 2 cups.
  3. Melt butter in shallow skillet or sauce pan. Cook onions and rice over medium heat until onions are tender and rice is golden brown, about 5 minutes. Add garlic, pepper and any other optional ingredients now, and cook for additional 1 minute.
  4. Turn heat to Med-High, Add liquid mixture to rice, stir briefly to mix in and bring to a boil.
  5. Once boiling, cover and reduce heat to a simmer for 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed.
  6. Keep covered and remove from heat, let sit for 5-10 minutes before serving
